## 1. Technology Futurists & AI Thinkers

### Amy Webb
- **Organization:** Future Today Institute (FTI) / Future Today Strategy Group (FTSG)
- **Key Publication:** Annual FTI Emerging Tech Trends Report; books *The Signals Are Talking*, *The Big Nine*, *The Genesis Machine*
- **URL:** https://futuretodayinstitute.com / https://ftsg.com
- **Known For:** Developed the CIPHER framework for identifying genuine long-term technology trends using quantitative, data-driven foresight. Her annual tech trends report is one of the most referenced in boardrooms worldwide.
- **Focus:** Global technology foresight -- AI, biotech, synthetic biology, geopolitics of tech
- **Why They Matter:** Her methodology is the gold standard for translating weak signals into actionable strategic intelligence. Named BBC 100 Women, Thinkers50 Radar.

### Ray Kurzweil
- **Organization:** Google (Director of Engineering)
- **Key Publication:** *The Singularity Is Near* (2005), *The Singularity Is Nearer* (2024), *How to Create a Mind*
- **URL:** https://www.kurzweilai.net
- **Known For:** Pioneering predictions on AI singularity, exponential growth of computing, and human-machine convergence. Famous for his long-term prediction accuracy rate.
- **Focus:** AI, transhumanism, exponential technologies
- **Why They Matter:** His predictions on computing, AI, and biotechnology set the intellectual framework for long-range technology forecasting. Influences policy and corporate R&D planning.

### Mustafa Suleyman
- **Organization:** Microsoft AI (CEO)
- **Key Publication:** *The Coming Wave: Technology, Power, and the Twenty-First Century's Greatest Dilemma* (2023)
- **URL:** https://www.thecomingwave.com
- **Known For:** Co-founded DeepMind and Inflection AI. One of the most prominent voices on AI governance, containment, and the societal impact of general-purpose AI.
- **Focus:** AI governance, AI safety, technology containment
- **Why They Matter:** Sits at the intersection of building frontier AI systems and articulating the governance challenge. His "containment problem" framing has become central to AI policy discussions.

### Kai-Fu Lee
- **Organization:** Sinovation Ventures
- **Key Publication:** *AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order* (2018), *AI 2041* (2021)
- **URL:** https://www.sinovationventures.com
- **Known For:** Former head of Google China. Leading voice on the US-China AI competition and how AI will reshape economies, particularly in Asia. Investor in 50+ AI unicorns.
- **Focus:** China tech ecosystem, AI commercialization, US-China tech competition
- **Why They Matter:** Provides the most authoritative bridge perspective between Western and Chinese AI ecosystems. Essential for understanding the geopolitics of AI.

### Ian Beacraft
- **Organization:** Signal and Cipher (CEO & Chief Futurist)
- **Key Publication:** Signal and Cipher content, SXSW and conference keynotes
- **URL:** https://www.signalandcipher.com
- **Known For:** Making bleeding-edge technology understandable for brands and organizations. Has used a "Virtual Ian" AI avatar to scale his presence. Focuses on practical AI futures for marketing and business.
- **Focus:** AI for business, brand strategy, emerging tech adoption
- **Why They Matter:** Bridges the gap between frontier AI research and practical brand/business strategy. Highly visible at SXSW and major industry events.

### Azeem Azhar
- **Organization:** Exponential View (Founder)
- **Key Publication:** *Exponential View* newsletter (Substack) and podcast; book *Exponential Age* (2021)
- **URL:** https://www.exponentialview.co
- **Known For:** Coined the concept of the "exponential gap" -- the widening divide between fast-moving technology and slow-adapting institutions. His newsletter reaches hundreds of thousands of subscribers.
- **Focus:** AI, exponential technologies, institutional adaptation, global tech trends
- **Why They Matter:** One of the most widely read technology analysts globally. Featured in The Economist, WSJ, Financial Times. Essential reading for executives navigating tech disruption.

### Peter Diamandis
- **Organization:** XPRIZE Foundation (Founder & Executive Chairman), Singularity University
- **Key Publication:** *Abundance*, *Bold*, *The Future Is Faster Than You Think*
- **URL:** https://www.diamandis.com
- **Known For:** Created the XPRIZE model for incentive-driven innovation. Advocates for a future of abundance driven by exponential technologies. Co-founded Singularity University with Ray Kurzweil.
- **Focus:** Abundance mindset, moonshot thinking, longevity, space
- **Why They Matter:** His organizations have trained thousands of corporate leaders in exponential thinking. The XPRIZE model has influenced how governments and philanthropies structure innovation incentives.

## 2. Business, Strategy & Innovation Thinkers

### Scott Galloway
- **Organization:** NYU Stern School of Business (Clinical Professor of Marketing)
- **Key Publication:** *Prof G Podcast*, *No Mercy / No Malice* newsletter; books *The Four*, *Post Corona*, *Adrift*
- **URL:** https://www.profgalloway.com
- **Known For:** Provocative analysis of Big Tech market power, wealth inequality, and generational economic shifts. Known for accurate predictions on tech industry dynamics.
- **Focus:** Big Tech power, market concentration, generational economics, higher education disruption
- **Why They Matter:** Reaches millions through podcast and newsletter. His frameworks on platform economics and wealth concentration influence corporate strategy and public policy debates.

### Mariana Mazzucato
- **Organization:** UCL Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose (IIPP), Founding Director
- **Key Publication:** *The Entrepreneurial State*, *Mission Economy*, *The Big Con*
- **URL:** https://marianamazzucato.com
- **Known For:** Reframed the debate on public vs. private sector innovation. Argues governments should be entrepreneurial actors, not just market-failure fixers. Appointed CBE in 2025.
- **Focus:** Innovation economics, mission-oriented policy, the role of the state in shaping markets
- **Why They Matter:** Advises governments worldwide on industrial strategy. Her "mission economy" framework has been adopted by the EU and multiple national governments for post-pandemic recovery and green transition.

### Carlota Perez
- **Organization:** UCL IIPP (Honorary Professor), London School of Economics
- **Key Publication:** *Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital: The Dynamics of Bubbles and Golden Ages* (2002)
- **URL:** https://www.carlotaperez.org
- **Known For:** Theory of techno-economic paradigm shifts explaining how technology revolutions interact with financial capital in predictable long-wave cycles. Co-founded The Other Canon for heterodox economics.
- **Focus:** Long-wave economics, technology revolutions, financial capital dynamics
- **Why They Matter:** Her framework explains why we see speculative bubbles followed by deployment periods after each major technology revolution. Essential for understanding where AI sits in the current tech cycle.

### Klaus Schwab
- **Organization:** World Economic Forum (WEF), Founder
- **Key Publication:** *The Fourth Industrial Revolution* (2017), *Stakeholder Capitalism*, *The Great Narrative*
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org
- **Known For:** Founded and led Davos for 50+ years. Coined "The Fourth Industrial Revolution" and "stakeholder capitalism." Stepped down as WEF chairman in 2025.
- **Focus:** Global governance, public-private cooperation, industrial revolutions
- **Why They Matter:** The WEF's Davos convening and Global Risks Report set the annual agenda for thousands of corporate and political leaders. The "Fourth Industrial Revolution" framing has been adopted by governments worldwide.

### Jonathan Brill
- **Organization:** Center for Radical Change (Head of Strategic Innovation), Singularity University
- **Key Publication:** *Rogue Waves: Future-Proof Your Business to Survive and Profit from Radical Change* (2021)
- **URL:** https://www.jonathanbrill.com
- **Known For:** Expert on strategic foresight and innovation under radical uncertainty. Former Global Futurist at HP. Helps organizations prepare for low-probability, high-impact disruptions.
- **Focus:** Strategic innovation, risk anticipation, radical change scenarios
- **Why They Matter:** Bridges academic foresight and corporate innovation practice. His "rogue waves" framework helps boards and C-suites prepare for cascading disruptions.

## 3. Geopolitical & Economic Strategists

### Ian Bremmer
- **Organization:** Eurasia Group (President & Founder), GZERO Media
- **Key Publication:** Annual *Top Risks Report*; books *The Power of Crisis*, *Us vs. Them*, *Every Nation for Itself*
- **URL:** https://www.eurasiagroup.net / https://www.gzeromedia.com
- **Known For:** Founded the world's largest political risk consultancy. His annual Top Risks report is the most-read geopolitical forecast in finance and government. Created the G-Zero concept.
- **Focus:** Geopolitical risk, US-China relations, technology geopolitics, democratic backsliding
- **Why They Matter:** The Eurasia Group Top Risks report sets the geopolitical agenda for the year. His frameworks are used by sovereign wealth funds, multinational CEOs, and intelligence agencies globally.

### Peter Zeihan
- **Organization:** Zeihan on Geopolitics (Founder)
- **Key Publication:** *The End of the World Is Just the Beginning*, *The Accidental Superpower*, *Disunited Nations*; daily video analysis
- **URL:** https://zeihan.com
- **Known For:** Demographic and geographic determinism -- argues that geography, demography, and energy access are the primary drivers of geopolitical power. Former VP at Stratfor.
- **Focus:** Deglobalization, demographics, energy geopolitics, supply chain restructuring
- **Why They Matter:** His framework on demographic collapse and deglobalization has become one of the most widely discussed geopolitical narratives. Advises U.S. military, energy majors, and financial institutions. NYT bestselling author endorsed by Bremmer, Zakaria, and Romney.

### Parag Khanna
- **Organization:** AlphaGeo / Climate Alpha (Founder & CEO), FutureMap
- **Key Publication:** *Connectography*, *The Future is Asian*, *MOVE*
- **URL:** https://www.paragkhanna.com
- **Known For:** Maps global connectivity infrastructure as the new geopolitical reality. Named one of Esquire's "75 Most Influential People of the 21st Century." WEF Young Global Leader. Advised the U.S. National Intelligence Council.
- **Focus:** Global connectivity, Asia's rise, climate migration, smart cities
- **Why They Matter:** His "connectography" thesis reframes geopolitics around infrastructure and supply chains rather than borders. Now applying AI/geospatial analytics to climate-driven migration and real estate risk.

### George Friedman
- **Organization:** Geopolitical Futures (Founder & Chairman)
- **Key Publication:** *The Next 100 Years*, *The Storm Before the Calm*, Geopolitical Futures weekly analysis
- **URL:** https://geopoliticalfutures.com
- **Known For:** Long-range geopolitical forecasting combining geography, economics, and military strategy. Founded Stratfor before launching Geopolitical Futures.
- **Focus:** Long-cycle geopolitical forecasting, U.S. grand strategy, Eurasian power dynamics
- **Why They Matter:** His macro-level, decades-long forecasts challenge conventional geopolitical wisdom and are widely read in defense and intelligence communities.

### Yuval Noah Harari
- **Organization:** Hebrew University of Jerusalem (Professor), Sapienship (Co-founder)
- **Key Publication:** *Sapiens*, *Homo Deus*, *21 Lessons for the 21st Century*, *Nexus* (2024)
- **URL:** https://www.ynharari.com
- **Known For:** Bridging history, philosophy, and technology to examine existential questions about humanity's future. His books have sold over 45 million copies in 65 languages.
- **Focus:** History of humankind, AI and consciousness, information networks, existential risk
- **Why They Matter:** One of the most widely read public intellectuals globally. His framing of AI as a potential end to "human history as we know it" shapes how political leaders and the public think about technology risk.

### Nassim Nicholas Taleb
- **Organization:** NYU Tandon School of Engineering (Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering)
- **Key Publication:** *The Black Swan*, *Antifragile*, *Skin in the Game*, *Incerto* series
- **URL:** https://www.fooledbyrandomness.com
- **Known For:** Developed the concepts of "Black Swan" events, antifragility, and "skin in the game." His work on fat-tailed risk distributions has influenced finance, policy, and risk management globally.
- **Focus:** Risk, uncertainty, probability, resilience under stress
- **Why They Matter:** His frameworks are used by central banks, hedge funds, and governments for stress-testing scenarios. "Black Swan" entered the mainstream lexicon and changed how organizations think about tail risk.

### Nouriel Roubini
- **Organization:** Roubini Macro Associates (CEO), NYU Stern (Professor Emeritus)
- **Key Publication:** *MegaThreats: Ten Dangerous Trends That Imperil Our Future* (2022), Roubini Macro analysis
- **URL:** https://nourielroubini.com
- **Known For:** Predicted the 2008 financial crisis (earning the nickname "Dr. Doom"). Now focuses on the convergence of mega-threats including debt, deglobalization, AI disruption, and climate change.
- **Focus:** Macroeconomic risk, financial crises, mega-threats convergence
- **Why They Matter:** His "mega-threats" framework captures how multiple systemic risks are compounding simultaneously. Essential for financial risk assessment and sovereign strategy.

### Daron Acemoglu
- **Organization:** MIT (Institute Professor of Economics)
- **Key Publication:** *Power and Progress* (2023, with Simon Johnson), *Why Nations Fail*, *The Narrow Corridor*
- **URL:** https://economics.mit.edu/people/faculty/daron-acemoglu
- **Known For:** Leading economist on how institutions shape technology's impact on society. Argues that AI's current trajectory is biased toward automation over augmentation, with negative labor market consequences.
- **Focus:** Institutional economics, technology and labor, AI policy, inequality
- **Why They Matter:** His task-based framework for AI and labor is shaping how the MIT Shaping the Future of Work Initiative and policymakers globally think about AI regulation and worker protection.

## 4. Sustainability & Climate Thinkers

### Christiana Figueres
- **Organization:** Global Optimism (Co-founder), The Earthshot Prize Foundation (Chair)
- **Key Publication:** *The Future We Choose: Surviving the Climate Crisis*; *Outrage + Optimism* podcast
- **URL:** https://www.globaloptimism.com/christiana-figueres
- **Known For:** As Executive Secretary of the UNFCCC, she architected the diplomatic effort that produced the 2015 Paris Agreement. Now drives climate action through narrative and optimism.
- **Focus:** Climate diplomacy, Paris Agreement implementation, climate narrative
- **Why They Matter:** The Paris Agreement is the defining climate governance framework. Her ongoing advocacy and podcast shape how the business and policy worlds think about climate action feasibility.

### Johan Rockstrom
- **Organization:** Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (Director)
- **Key Publication:** Planetary Boundaries framework (2009, updated); *Breaking Boundaries* (documentary/book)
- **URL:** https://www.pik-potsdam.de/members/johanro
- **Known For:** Defined the nine planetary boundaries framework that quantifies Earth's safe operating space for humanity. His science directly informs the EU Green Deal and global sustainability targets.
- **Focus:** Planetary boundaries, Earth system science, climate tipping points
- **Why They Matter:** The planetary boundaries framework is the scientific foundation for global sustainability policy. Used by the UN, EU, and hundreds of companies to set science-based targets.

### Kate Raworth
- **Organization:** Doughnut Economics Action Lab (DEAL), Co-founder; University of Oxford & Amsterdam
- **Key Publication:** *Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist* (2017)
- **URL:** https://www.kateraworth.com / https://doughnuteconomics.org
- **Known For:** Created the "Doughnut" visual framework -- a social foundation of human needs within an ecological ceiling of planetary boundaries. Adopted by Amsterdam, Barcelona, and other cities as a governance model.
- **Focus:** Post-growth economics, regenerative economics, social-planetary boundaries
- **Why They Matter:** Her Doughnut model is being adopted by cities, businesses, and institutions worldwide as an alternative to GDP-growth-centric thinking. Bridges economics, sustainability, and social justice.
